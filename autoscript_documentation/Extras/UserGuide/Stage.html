User guide: Stage

This chapter shows how to perform absolute or relative movements with the microscope stage. Movements can be performed in a normal or safe manner - resetting the tilts before the movement and restoring them after the movement to reduce the likelihood of pole contact.

Note: Distances are in meters and tilts are in radians.

Before proceeding with the following code snippets, make sure to initialize AutoScript.

Move to an absolute position of 100 µm in the X axis and 213 µm in the Y axis. The Z, A and B axes are not changed: 


        microscope.specimen.stage.absolute_move(StagePosition(x=100e-6, y=213e-6))

Tilt to A = 30° and B = 4°


        import math
        
        p = StagePosition(a=math.radians(30), b=math.radians(4))
        microscope.specimen.stage.absolute_move(p)

Read the stage position and inspect it:


        print(microscope.specimen.stage.position)
        
        > StagePosition(x=0.000123, y=0.000456, z=2.34e-05, a=0.17453293)

Perform a relative movement by -20 µm in the Z axis:


        p = StagePosition(z=-20.0e-6)
        microscope.specimen.stage.relative_move(p)

Get the holder type:


        print(microscope.specimen.stage.get_holder_type())
        
        > DoubleTilt
